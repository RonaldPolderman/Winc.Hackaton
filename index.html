This WEBSITE is about my Kickboxing journey.

The journey is about to start

Around the age of 40 i had a serious appendix operation and was hospitalised for around 3 weeks.
A great reason to stop smoking because, well, i couldn't cause i was bed ridden. So far so good you might say but i started gaining weight like crazy.
The peak of my weight was around 220 pounds / 100kg and the people close to me had to take a bycicle to get around me.

The journey of a 1000 miles starts with the first step.

At 47 enough was enough and a close co-worker and i started intermittent fasting. It's more a lifestyle then it is a diet altho eating and drinking healthy along with the intermittent fasting doesn't slow the process of losing weight obviously.
There are different ways to IF but we followed the 16-8 pattern. This means as much as dont eat 16 hours and then in the next 8 hours you eat normal portions so don't overeat.
My regular consumption was something along the lines of:

  1. Breakfast: fruit
    • banana
    • apple
    • orange
  2. Dinner: preferably a filler with as less (or slower) carbohydrates as possible stuff like:
    • Quinoa
    • Sweet potato
    • Bulgur rice
  3. An evening snack usually 1 or 2 crackers with some lean topping.
I tried as much as possible to avoid bread, potatoes, pasta's and rice. The problem is however that it ain't that hard to know what not to eat but to keep things interesting and with some degree of variation is harder.

More about Intermittend Fasting in this link.
Or you can watch this YouTube video.

From the start of IF till 2 years in i pretty much drank nothing but water. Obviously cut out any additional sugars because ... yeah.... they are great for your lazy brain but bad for your health ( don't get me wrong i love sugars and candy but that don't make em any better for your health)

Success is a journey not a destination.

Alongside the dabble into IF i always had an interest in martial arts. Kickboxing and Muay Thai in particular.
About a 3 minute walk away there was a gym who teached kickboxing. Pancration, the name of the school, was founded by Chris Dolman and Thom Harinck. Both well known legends in the sport.
More about Chris and Thom here and here.
One of the sensei was the famous champion maker Lucien Carbin along with two other trainers Sergio and Cedric.
This is one of my favorite video's of Lucien Carbin with one of his pupils at Pancration Lucien Carbin and Pricilla. To take confusion away, Pricilla is the one with hair.
And for more data about Lucien this is his wikipedia page. I started training once a week, just some bag work with the beginner class sensei Cedric. My stamina level was horrible and i could barely finish an hour. Even tho i was exhausted at the end of the class i really did enjoy the intensity of the workout.
about 6 months in the game got me hooked, i started going to the heavy bag trainings 3 times a week and felt i was getting more confident with throwing my hands and feet.
Besides my confident level rising my bodyfat level was plummeting. This was the sign for me to go all out, the sport was addictive, i went from 3 to 4 to 5 to as much as i possibly could go. Sometimes twice a day.
I'm hooked in a possitive way to the sport now. I went to Thailand to train for a few weeks in 2020 just before all hell broke loose with Covid. Due to some unfortunate stuff Pancration had to close it's doors at the original location and is moving around since.
Even tho i'm still training at Pancration i tried some other gyms too.

My page will be about the gym's i trained at and some of my personal stuff.
